Strip Steaks au Poivre

Servings: 4

Total Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 (1½-inch thick) New York strip steaks (about 1½ pounds total)
  • 2½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 large shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 thyme sprigs
  • ½ cup cognac, dry sherry, or brandy
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Flaky sea salt, for serving

Instructions

  1. Pat 2 New York strip steaks dry with paper towels. Season all over with 2½ teaspoons kosher salt and a generous amount of ground black pepper. Set aside for at least 15 minutes.
  2. Finely chop 1 large shallot. Thinly slice 2 garlic cloves. Firmly smash 2 additional garlic cloves.
  3. Place 1 tablespoon whole peppercorns in a sealable plastic bag, seal the bag, and crush with the bottom of a small saucepan or heavy pan until coarsely cracked.
  4. Heat a 12-inch cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat for 4 minutes.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons vegetable oil to the skillet. Add the steaks and cook, undisturbed, until a deep golden brown crust forms, about 3 minutes. Flip and cook the other side until equally golden, about 3 more minutes. (Optional: Upend steaks on their sides to brown the edges, about 2 to 3 more minutes).
  6. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add 1 tablespoon butter, 3 thyme sprigs, and the 2 smashed garlic cloves. Use a spoon to baste the steaks continuously for about 2 minutes, until they reach 120°F. Transfer to a cutting board to rest.
  7. Add 2 tablespoons butter to the skillet along with the shallot, sliced garlic, and crushed peppercorns. Cook, stirring often, until shallot is translucent and mixture is not browned, 4 to 5 minutes.
  8. Turn off the heat and add ½ cup cognac. Return to medium heat and cook until cognac is mostly evaporated and spoon leaves streaks in skillet when stirring, 1 to 2 minutes.
  9. Add ½ cup heavy cream, bring to a simmer, and cook until it coats the back of a spoon, about 1 minute. Season with kosher salt.
  10. Slice the steak against the grain. Transfer to a serving platter.
  11. Pour any resting juices back into the skillet and stir into the sauce. Spoon sauce generously over steak, season with flaky sea salt, and serve immediately.
